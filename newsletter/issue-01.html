The Federal Style Collection

I · As received

  

II · Restored

Family heirlooms, passed down to our client and destined for a family member’s new Washington, DC apartment. The finish arrived completely worn in areas; the veneer and inlay were beautiful but very thin, fragile, and missing in places. From the start, we knew these pieces needed extra care.

After hand stripping, cleaning and sanding, you could really see the beauty of the grain. From a few stain options applied to the piece itself — never a sample card — the client chose a mahogany that brought out the depth of the wood and the contrasting tones in the grain. Sealer, sanded smooth; then three coats of a high-quality, non-yellowing lacquer, so the collection has the protection it needs for the next generation.

Care at home

Put down the Pledge.

Most supermarket furniture polishes contain silicone. They look great at first — and then, over time, the silicone builds a slick surface tension that turns blotchy and smudges whenever it’s handled. House cleaners love the stuff because it makes everything shiny; make sure you know exactly what is being used on your fine pieces.

Instead: dust and wipe with a barely damp cotton cloth, with the grain. Once or twice a year, a quality cream polish will lift the buildup of regular use and bring back the luster.
