01 — Case Study · The Federal CollectionWhitfield · for a new Washington, DC apartment

Featured restoration

The Federal Style Collection

Unique and hard to find — family heirlooms passed down to our client, who wanted them restored for a family member’s new DC apartment.

The finish arrived completely worn in areas. The veneer and inlay are very beautiful but very thin and fragile, and missing in a few places — from the start, we knew these pieces needed extra care. After hand stripping the finish, cleaning and sanding, you could really see the beauty of the grain.

From a few stain options applied to the piece itself, the client chose a mahogany finish that brought out the depth of the wood and the contrasting tones in the grain. Sealer went on and was sanded down to a smooth, even surface — then three coats of a high-quality, non-yellowing lacquer, so the collection has the protection it needs for the next generation.

Hand stripped · Stain chosen on the piece · Sealed & sanded · Non-yellowing lacquer, three coats

02 — Case Study · The SecretarySlant-front · ball-and-claw feet

Case study

The Chippendale secretary.

It came in blackened — decades of darkened finish and grime hiding everything down to the ball-and-claw feet.

Stripped by hand and partly disassembled on the bench, the case gave up its secret: figured mahogany that no one living had ever seen. Re-glued, refinished and polished, it went home glowing — and was photographed there, on the client’s own hardwood floor.

Hand stripped · Disassembled & re-glued · Figured mahogany revealed

03 — Case Study · The Writing DeskFrench parquetry · brass pulls

Case study

The kidney writing desk.

A French kidney-shaped writing desk, its top badly water-stained and blotchy, the finish worn through.

Hand stripping took it back to pale, raw wood — and uncovered parquetry drawer fronts worth every hour of care. Refinished in warm mahogany with the brass pulls polished, the desk reads as one piece again: stains gone, pattern crisp, surface ready for another lifetime of letters.

Hand stripped · Parquetry preserved · Warm mahogany · Brass polished

04 — The Bench BookFour rescues · before & after

From the showcase

Four more from the bench book.

Not every rescue needs three plates. These four came in wrecked, worn or unreadable — and went home restored.

Structural repair

The rebuilt lowboy.

It arrived as a puzzle: one side smashed through, all four cabriole legs snapped off, the case lying in pieces. We rebuilt the structure, re-set the legs and refinished the mahogany — the carved shell drawer and Queen Anne lines exactly where they belong.

Case rebuiltFour legs re-setRefinished mahogany

Veneer & inlay

The marquetry commode.

Under a dark, muddy finish, the floral marquetry of this European serpentine commode was barely readable. Careful hand stripping — never tanks, never acids — brought back the burl walnut and every crisp line of the inlay.

Hand strippedMarquetry revivedBurl walnut refinished

Fine detail

The moon-gazing chair.

A Chinese folding chair, its mother-of-pearl inlay of butterflies and blossoms dulled under years of grime. Cleaned and polished entirely by hand, the rosewood came back deep and glossy — and the inlay caught the light again.

Hand cleanedRosewood polishedInlay preserved

Heirloom rescue

The child’s high chair.

A Victorian convertible high chair — the kind built to feed one generation, rock the next and be handed to a third. It arrived with the wood crumbling and the cane seat blown out. We rebuilt it: fresh hand caning, the feeding tray back in place, the rocker mechanism working again.
