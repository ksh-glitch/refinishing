Notable commissions · Since 1991

A register of work held in trust.

The pieces on our benches arrive from reading rooms and chancelleries, clubhouses and hotel lobbies, and from private homes across Washington, Maryland and Virginia. What follows is a plain accounting of some of the names this workshop has served.

01 — ProvenanceWhy the call comes here

Why institutions call

Chosen for the hands.

Institutions choose a restorer much the way collectors do — for the quality of the hand work, and for the quiet around it.

Everything here is worked at a bench, by the same shop that has answered the phone since 1991. We are discreet about what passes through the workshop, and we collect and return pieces ourselves — free pick-up and delivery across DC, Maryland and Virginia — which matters as much to a chancery as it does to a household.

02 — The RegisterSet in four columns

The names

The register, in four columns.

  1. Government & institutions

    The Library of Congress; the Environmental Protection Agency; the National PTA.

  2. The embassies

    The Washington embassies of Britain, Lithuania, France, Egypt, Denmark, Moldova, Poland, Japan, Spain, Malta and Guatemala.

  3. Clubs & hospitality

    Washington Golf & Country Club; Trump National Golf Club Washington; the Hyatt Reston; Signature Flight Support.

  4. Private collections

    The households of Washington, Maryland and Virginia, and the designers who furnish them — names we leave off the printed page.

† A register, not a complete accounting.

03 — Commerce & ClaimsBeyond the household

Commercial & insurance work

Work at a larger scale.

The bench does not mind the size of the client. In October 2016 the shop refinished the wooden lobby walls of a Pennsylvania Avenue building — architectural woodwork, brought to the same standard as the furniture.

We also take on insurance work. When fire, smoke or water reaches fine furniture, we restore the claimed pieces — the same hand methods, applied under a claim rather than a commission.
