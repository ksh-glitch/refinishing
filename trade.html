For the trade · Designers · Estates · Institutions

Furniture restoration for Washington’s trade.

Interior designers, estate stewards and institutions across Washington DC, Maryland and Virginia have sent work to this bench for decades. The arrangement is simple: your client relationship stays yours, the hand work stays ours, and the piece comes back ready for the room it was chosen for.

01 — The DesignersOne workroom, both trades

Working with interior designers

One workroom, show-wood and cloth.

Most upholstery shops send the woodwork out; most refinishers send the fabric out. In our Sterling workroom the same small, handpicked team does both — a loose joint re-glued, a cracked leg rebuilt, the show-wood hand polished before a single yard of new fabric goes on.

Bring the fabric your client has fallen for — client-supplied material is welcome here — or let us help source one suited to the piece and the room. And when a piece must sit beside furniture it wasn’t born with, we match stain from photographs of the pieces it needs to live alongside, or you visit the shop and choose on the stripped wood itself, sample by sample.

Discretion is part of the service. Our register of private clients — the households of Washington and the designers who furnish them — stays off the printed page.

02 — The InstitutionsGovernment · Embassies · Clubs

Estates & institutions

The furniture of formal rooms.

The Library of Congress, the Environmental Protection Agency, eleven Washington embassies, and the clubs and hotels of the region have all sent furniture to this shop. In October 2016 the bench went to the building: the wooden lobby walls of a Pennsylvania Avenue address, refinished to the same standard as the furniture.

The full register is kept on the notable commissions page.

03 — Claims & CommerceSince the 1990s

Estates, insurers & commercial accounts

Paperwork an adjuster can trust.

When an estate or a claim needs more than craftsmanship — when it needs a record — we have been the quiet third party at the table since the 1990s.

We document the damage we find, photograph the piece at every stage, and quote what the restoration actually requires: no more, no less. Everyone at the table — owner, insurer, adjuster — looks at the same facts.

For commercial accounts, the arrangement scales: from touch-up to full restoration for delivery and moving companies, re-location companies, hotels, hospitals, retirement homes, offices and banks — the same hand work, at competitive rates.

04 — TermsHow an engagement runs

The terms, plainly

How the trade works with us.

  1. Photographs first

    Send a few photos and a short description of each piece. Estimates are always free, and the reply is quick and honest — including when the honest answer is that a piece isn’t worth the cost of restoring.

  2. Logistics, quoted

    Pick-up and delivery are available throughout Washington DC, Maryland and Virginia, quoted with your estimate — we come to the residence or the site for both ends of the job.

  3. Every step by hand

    Never acids, never high-pressure water, never dipping tanks. Fragile veneer, inlay and joinery survive because the piece comes down to bare wood the slow way.

  4. Paid on delivery

    No money up front. We’re paid when the work is delivered and you — and your client — are more than satisfied with it.
