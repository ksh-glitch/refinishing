Fine furniture care 101 · From the bench

Care for it like the people who fix it.

Most of the damage that reaches our bench was preventable. Taking good care of quality wood furniture is easier than people think — done properly, your pieces will be enjoyed for generations. Here is what thirty-five years of undoing mistakes has taught us.

01 — The Cardinal RulePolishes

Avoid most advertised polishes

Put down the Pledge.

Most supermarket furniture polishes contain silicone. They look great at first — and then, over time, the silicone builds a slick surface tension that turns blotchy and smudges whenever it’s handled.

House cleaners love the stuff because it makes everything shiny. Have a conversation with yours, and make sure you know exactly what is being used on your fine pieces.

Use this instead: for any fine finish, dust and wipe with a damp cotton cloth. Once or twice a year, a quality cream polish — we recommend Mohawk’s Oz Cream Polish — will lift the buildup of regular use and bring back the luster. It works wonders on wooden kitchen cabinets, too.

02 — Daily HabitsFive rules

The habits that matter

Five rules from the workshop.

  1. Dust regularly, with the grain

    Use a clean, soft or microfiber cloth — barely dampened, never wet — and always work in the direction of the grain. Don’t forget hidden surfaces: the tops of wardrobes and cabinets collect dust that affects air quality. A workshop trick: line those tops with parchment or newspaper, and simply replace it when it’s done its job.

  2. Lift, don’t drag

    Most small scratches come from sliding objects across a surface. Lift instead. Use protective pads under plastics, warm items and vases — and rotate your accessories so they don’t sit in one spot forever.

  3. Blot, don’t wipe

    Accidents happen. Clean spills immediately with an absorbent cloth and a blotting action — wiping spreads the liquid across the finish. You are working against the clock: liquids left standing damage finishes, and alcohol, perfume or nail polish can cause severe damage that needs professional attention. Minimizing it is always worth the try.

  4. Keep the climate steady

    Drastic swings in temperature or humidity damage wood quickly. Avoid damp rooms, and never use a storage facility that isn’t temperature- and humidity-controlled.

  5. Know when to call a professional

    Once or twice a year, the right polish or wax — matched to your specific finish — keeps a piece healthy. Your local furniture refinisher can tell you exactly what your finish needs. We’re happy to be that call: 703-437-7446.
